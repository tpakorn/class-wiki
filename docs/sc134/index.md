# SC134 · Physics for Engineers II

**A knowledge wiki for the electromagnetism half of SC134** — Thammasat University,
semester 2. From two rubbed amber rods to Maxwell's equations and light, in fourteen
lectures. (Optics and modern physics follow the midterm with the second-half
instructors.)

## The course in one paragraph

Electromagnetism is one long unification. [Charge](concepts/electric-charge.md)
creates [fields](concepts/electric-field.md); [Gauss's law](concepts/gauss-law.md)
organizes them; [potential](concepts/electric-potential.md) makes them computable;
[capacitors](concepts/capacitance.md) store them and [circuits](concepts/dc-circuits.md)
put them to work. Then the twist: *moving* charge makes
[magnetic fields](concepts/magnetic-field.md)
([Biot–Savart](concepts/biot-savart-law.md), [Ampère](concepts/amperes-law.md)), and
*changing* magnetic fields make electric fields back
([Faraday](concepts/faraday-law.md)). Close the loop with Maxwell's displacement
current and the equations sing:
[electromagnetic waves](concepts/electromagnetic-waves.md), moving at exactly the
measured speed of light.

    potential needs field, circuits need potential, induction needs flux again. If a
    late topic feels hard, the [concept graph](concept-graph.md) will point at the
    early link that needs repair. The prequel course is
    [SC133 mechanics](../physics1/index.md); the field mathematics returns with full
    rigor in [PHY621](../phy621/index.md).
