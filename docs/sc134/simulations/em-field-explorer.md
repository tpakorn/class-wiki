# E&M Field Explorer

## Learning goal

Make [electric fields](../concepts/electric-field.md) *tangible*: field lines leave
positive charges and land on negative ones; where lines crowd, $|\vec E|$ is large;
the [potential](../concepts/electric-potential.md) (background shading) is high near
positive charges and its steepest descent *is* the field.

## Things to try

1. **Dipole preset** — compare near-field (loops from + to −) with far-field
   (looks like a weak single charge... but falls as $1/r^3$:
   [electric dipole](../concepts/electric-dipole.md)).
2. **Like pair** — find the dead spot between them where $\vec E = 0$, and notice no
   field line passes through it.
3. **Build a "capacitor"** — a row of + above a row of −: watch the field become
   uniform between and weak outside, exactly the
   [parallel-plate idealization](../concepts/capacitance.md).
4. **Count line endings** — lines per charge is proportional to $|q|$; that
   bookkeeping *is* [Gauss's law](../concepts/gauss-law.md).
