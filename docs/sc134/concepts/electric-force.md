# Electric Force on Charges

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec2](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

A charge placed in an existing field feels a force proportional to its charge magnitude.

## Formal Definition

$$\vec{F} = q\vec{E}$$

## Mathematical Formulation

For a dipole in uniform $\vec{E}$: $\vec{\tau} = \vec{p}\times\vec{E}$, $U = -\vec{p}\cdot\vec{E}$.

## Derivation

Definition of field; for a dipole integrate force on $+q$ and $-q$ separated by $\vec{d}$.

## Worked Example

Electron in $1\,\text{N/C}$ field feels $1.6\times10^{-19}\,\text{N}$ opposite to field direction.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating field as a force.
- Getting dipole torque direction wrong.
