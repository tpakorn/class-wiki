# Electric Current

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec1, Lec8](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

Current is the flow rate of charge—like water current, but with electrons (or ions).

## Formal Definition

$$I = \frac{dq}{dt}$$

## Mathematical Formulation

Current density: $\vec{J} = nq\vec{v}_d$
Drift speed: $v_d = I/(nqA)$

## Derivation

Count charges passing a cross-section per unit time; multiply by charge magnitude.

## Worked Example

2 A through a wire of area $1\,\text{mm}^2$ with $n\approx 8.5\times10^{28}\,\text{m}^{-3}$ gives $v_d\approx 1.5\times10^{-4}\,\text{m/s}$.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing drift speed with signal speed.
- Using wrong cross-sectional area in $J$.
