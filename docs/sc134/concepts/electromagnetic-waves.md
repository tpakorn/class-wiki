# Electromagnetic Waves

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec14](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

Oscillating electric and magnetic fields sustain each other in vacuum, propagating at the speed of light.

## Formal Definition

Self-sustaining solutions to Maxwell's equations in free space with no charges or currents.

## Mathematical Formulation

Wave equation from Maxwell: $\nabla^2 E = \mu_0\varepsilon_0 \partial^2 E/\partial t^2$
Speed: $c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0} \approx 3.00\times10^8\,\text{m/s}$
Energy density: $u = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2 + \frac{1}{2\mu_0}B^2$

## Derivation

Take curl of Faraday's law, substitute Ampere-Maxwell, use $\nabla\cdot E = 0$ in vacuum.

## Worked Example

Radio waves, visible light, X-rays are all EM waves with different frequencies.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing phase between $E$ and $B$ (they are in phase in vacuum).
- Using SI units without $\mu_0\varepsilon_0$.
