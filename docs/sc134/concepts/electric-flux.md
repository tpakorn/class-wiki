# Electric Flux

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec4](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

Flux counts how many field lines pierce a surface—open surfaces measure flow, closed surfaces measure enclosed sources.

## Formal Definition

$$\Phi_E = \int \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A}$$

## Mathematical Formulation

Closed surface: $\oint \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = q_{\text{enc}}/\varepsilon_0$
Uniform field through flat area: $\Phi = EA\cos\theta$.

## Derivation

Parallel-plate model visualizes field lines; Gauss's law turns flux into enclosed charge.

## Worked Example

Cube in uniform $E$ parallel to edges has zero net flux if no charge inside.

## Common Mistakes

- Counting every field line instead of piercing component.
- Confusing open vs closed surface formulas.
