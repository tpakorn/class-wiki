# Magnetic Force

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec9](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

Only moving charges feel magnetic forces, and the force is perpendicular to both velocity and field.

## Formal Definition

Lorentz force: $\vec{F} = q\vec{v}\times\vec{B}$

## Mathematical Formulation

Magnitude: $F = qvB\sin\theta$
Direction: right-hand rule.
Cyclotron radius: $r = mv/(|q|B)$.

## Derivation

Cross product follows from relativistic transformation of electric fields; for current elements integrate $dq\vec{v}$.

## Worked Example

Electron ($v=10^6\,\text{m/s}$, $B=0.1\,\text{T}$) circles with $r\approx 5.7\times10^{-5}\,\text{m}$.

## Common Mistakes

- Using electric force formula for magnetic fields.
- Getting sign wrong for electrons.
