# Electric Charge

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec1](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

Charge is the source of electric forces. It comes in two flavors, positive and negative, and obeys strict conservation laws.

## Formal Definition

Electric charge $q$ is a scalar property of matter that produces and responds to electric fields. The elementary charge is $e=1.60\times10^{-19}\,\text{C}$.

## Mathematical Formulation

$$Q = ne$$
Coulomb's law: $$F = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$

## Derivation

Coulomb's law was determined experimentally by torsion balance. The $1/4\pi\varepsilon_0$ factor ensures consistent units in SI.

## Worked Example

Two protons 1 nm apart repel with $F\approx 2.3\times10^{-10}\,\text{N}$.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating charge as a vector.
- Forgoing the absolute value when computing magnitude.
