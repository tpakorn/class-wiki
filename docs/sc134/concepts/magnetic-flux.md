# Magnetic Flux

*Source lecture(s):* [SC134 Lec11](../lectures.md)

## Intuition

Flux measures how many magnetic field lines pass through a loop—it is the bridge between $B$ and induced EMF.

## Formal Definition

$$\Phi_B = \int \vec{B}\cdot d\vec{A}$$

## Mathematical Formulation

Uniform $B$ through flat loop: $\Phi_B = BA\cos\theta$
Unit: 1 weber (Wb) = 1 T·m$^2$.

## Derivation

Surface integral of normal component; analogous to electric flux but with $B$.

## Worked Example

Loop area $0.1\,\text{m}^2$ at $30^\circ$ to 0.5 T field: $\Phi = 0.5\times0.1\times\cos30^\circ \approx 0.043\,\text{Wb}$.

## Common Mistakes

- Using open surface instead of any surface bounded by loop.
- Forgetting $\cos\theta$.

## Quiz

**Q1.** Can flux change if $B$ is constant?

??? success "Answer"
    Yes—if the loop rotates or changes area.
