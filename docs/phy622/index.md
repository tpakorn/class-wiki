# PHY622 · Mathematical Methods in Physics II

**A knowledge wiki for PHY622** — Thammasat University graduate course, semester 2.
Three arcs of beautiful mathematics: variational calculus, complex analysis, and group
theory — each one a different answer to *"what does physics optimize, integrate, or
respect?"*

## The course in one paragraph

First arc: nature extremizes.
[Calculus of variations](concepts/calculus-of-variations.md) finds the curves that
make functionals stationary via the
[Euler–Lagrange equation](concepts/euler-lagrange-equation.md) —
[geodesics](concepts/geodesics.md), [soap films](concepts/minimal-surfaces.md), and
all of mechanics through [Hamilton's principle](concepts/hamilton-principle.md).
Second arc: [complex analyticity](concepts/analytic-functions.md) is a straitjacket so
tight ([Cauchy–Riemann](concepts/cauchy-riemann.md)) that a function's boundary values
determine its interior ([Cauchy's formula](concepts/cauchy-integral-formula.md)) and
impossible real integrals fall to the
[residue theorem](concepts/residue-theorem.md). Third arc: symmetry.
[Groups](concepts/symmetry-groups.md) and their
[representations](concepts/group-representations.md) organize everything from crystal
spectra to spin, culminating in [Lie groups](concepts/lie-groups.md) and the
double cover [SU(2) → SO(3)](concepts/su2-so3.md) behind the electron's 720°.
