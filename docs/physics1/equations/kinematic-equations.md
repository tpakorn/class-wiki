# Kinematic Equations


Constant-acceleration kinematics.

$$
v = v_0 + a t
$$
$$
x = x_0 + v_0 t + \tfrac12 a t^2
$$
$$
v^2 = v_0^2 + 2 a (x - x_0)
$$

**Variable table**

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $v$ | final velocity |
| $v_0$ | initial velocity |
| $a$ | acceleration |
| $t$ | time |
| $x$ | final position |
| $x_0$ | initial position |

**Assumptions**
- Constant acceleration
- Straight-line motion

**Derivation**
Integrate $a=$ constant once to obtain $v$, twice to obtain $x$.
