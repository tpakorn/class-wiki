# Collision Lab

## Learning goal

Watch [momentum](../concepts/linear-momentum.md) survive every collision while
kinetic energy survives only elastic ones — the green momentum bar never moves at
impact; the yellow energy bar drops by exactly the amount turned to heat.

## Things to try

1. **Equal masses, elastic (e = 1), target at rest:** the balls *swap* velocities —
   Newton's cradle in slow motion.
2. **Slide e → 0** (perfectly inelastic): they stick and move at the
   [center-of-mass velocity](../concepts/center-of-mass.md); note the KE loss —
   maximum allowed by momentum conservation.
3. **Heavy projectile, light target:** the light ball leaves at nearly *twice* the
   projectile speed. Reverse the masses: the projectile bounces back.
4. **Head-on** ($v_2 < 0$): total momentum can be *zero* while kinetic energy is
   large — vectors cancel, scalars don't.
