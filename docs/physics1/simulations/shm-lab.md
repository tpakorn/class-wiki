# Oscillator Lab

## Learning goal

Watch the three faces of the harmonic oscillator: free
[SHM](../concepts/simple-harmonic-motion.md) at
$\omega_0 = \sqrt{k/m}$, decay under [damping](../concepts/damped-oscillations.md),
and the resonance peak when driven near $\omega_0$.

## Things to try

1. **Zero damping, no drive:** pure SHM. Change $m$ and $k$ and verify the period
   follows $T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k}$ (heavier → slower, stiffer → faster).
2. **Add damping** and watch the envelope decay; push $b$ high enough and the
   oscillation dies without completing a cycle (overdamped).
3. **Drive it** with $\omega_d$ far below, at, and above $\omega_0$ (green marker on
   the response curve). At resonance, tiny forces build huge swings.
4. **Increase damping while driven at resonance** — the peak flattens: that's a lower
   $Q$.
