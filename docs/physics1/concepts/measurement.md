# Measurement

 measurement is the foundation of all experimental physics. It connects abstract physical quantities to reproducible standards.

## Intuition
Physics describes patterns in nature quantitatively. To test any claim, you must measure the quantities involved.

## Formal Definition
A measurement compares a physical quantity to a defined standard, yielding a numerical value with units.

## Mathematical Formulation
$$
Q = N \times u
$$
- $Q$ = physical quantity
- $N$ = measured number
- $u$ = unit

## Worked Example
The distance to a star is measured in light-years, while atomic spacing uses picometers. The unit must match the scale of the quantity.

## Common Mistakes
 Mixing unit systems without conversion factors. Treating significant figures as optional.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Why are SI units preferred in physics?
A: They provide a universal, reproducible standard.

Q: What is the difference between accuracy and precision?
A: Accuracy is closeness to the true value; precision is repeatability.
