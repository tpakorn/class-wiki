# Friction and Drag

Friction opposes relative motion at surfaces. Drag opposes motion through a fluid.

## Intuition
Rubbing your hands together feels warm — friction converts kinetic energy to heat.

## Formal Definition
Static friction: $f_s \le \mu_s N$
Kinetic friction: $f_k = \mu_k N$

Drag force:
$$
F_D = \tfrac12 \rho v^2 C_D A
$$

## Worked Example
A $10\,\text{N}$ block on a horizontal surface with $\mu_s = 0.5$ needs at least $5\,\text{N}$ to start sliding.

## Common Mistakes
Using kinetic friction coefficients for static situations.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Does friction always oppose motion?
A: It opposes relative motion or its tendency.
