# Center of Mass and Linear Momentum

The center of mass (CM) is the weighted average position of all mass.

## Intuition
A thrown wrench rotates about its CM while the CM follows the parabolic trajectory.

## Formal Definition
$$
\vec{r}_{CM} = \frac{1}{M}\sum m_i \vec{r}_i
$$

Linear momentum:
$$
\vec{p} = m\vec{v}
$$

 Momentum conservation holds when external force is zero.

## Worked Example
Two carts, $2\,\text{kg}$ at $3\,\text{m/s}$ and $3\,\text{kg}$ at $-2\,\text{m/s}$, have total momentum $-6\,\text{kg m/s}$.

## Common Mistakes
Confusing momentum conservation with energy conservation.
