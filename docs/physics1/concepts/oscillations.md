# Oscillations

Oscillations describe repeating motion around an equilibrium position.

## Intuition
A mass on a spring bounces back and forth because restoring force is proportional to displacement.

## Formal Definition
Simple harmonic oscillator:
$$
m\ddot{x} + kx = 0 \implies x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)
$$

Frequency:
$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}, \quad f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}
$$

## Worked Example
A spring with $k=400\,\text{N/m}$ and $m=0.25\,\text{kg}$ oscillates at $\omega \approx 40\,\text{rad/s}$.

## Common Mistakes
Forgetting the $2\pi$ conversion between angular and ordinary frequency.
