# Kinetic Energy and Work

Work transfers energy by force acting over displacement. Kinetic energy measures motion energy.

## Intuition
Pushing a stalled car adds energy to it. That energy is stored as kinetic energy.

## Formal Definition
Work:
$$
W = \int \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{x}
$$

Kinetic energy:
$$
K = \tfrac12 m v^2
$$

Work–energy theorem:
$$
W = \Delta K
$$

## Worked Example
A $1000\,\text{kg}$ car speeding up from $10$ to $20\,\text{m/s}$ requires $\Delta K = 150\,\text{kJ}$.

## Common Mistakes
Forgetting the dot product sign — only force components along displacement do work.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Is work done when carrying a box horizontally?
A: No — force is vertical while displacement is horizontal, so $\vec{F}\cdot\vec{d}=0$.
