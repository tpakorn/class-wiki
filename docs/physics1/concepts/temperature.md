# Temperature and Heat

Temperature measures average kinetic energy; heat is energy transferred due to temperature difference.

## Intuition
A hot cup warms your hands because thermal energy flows from the cup to cooler skin.

## Formal Definition
Ideal gas temperature relation:
$$
\overline{K} = \tfrac32 kT
$$

Heat added to a substance:
$$
Q = mc\Delta T
$$

## Worked Example
Heating $0.5\,\text{kg}$ water by $20\,\text{K}$ with $c=4186\,\text{J/(kg K)}$ requires $41{,}860\,\text{J}$.

## Common Mistakes
Using temperature difference in Celsius for $Q=mc\Delta T$ without noting that $\Delta °C = \Delta K$.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Does heat flow from cold to hot in nature?
A: No — spontaneously it flows from hot to cold.
