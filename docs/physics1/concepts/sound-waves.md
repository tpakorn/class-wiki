# Sound Waves

Sound is a longitudinal pressure wave in air or other media.

## Intuition
Speaker cones push air, creating compressions and rarefactions that travel to your ear.

## Formal Definition
Wave speed in air:
$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{\gamma R T}{M}}
$$

## Worked Example
At $20°\text{C}$, $v \approx 343\,\text{m/s}$ in dry air.

## Common Mistakes
Using the wrong temperature when computing speed from $\sqrt{T}$.

Q: Is sound a transverse wave in air?
A: No — it is longitudinal.
