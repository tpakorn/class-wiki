# Orbits

Orbits arise when gravity provides exactly the centripetal force needed for continuous curved motion.

## Intuition
Throw something fast enough horizontally and it keeps missing the ground — that’s orbit.

## Formal Definition
Orbital speed:
$$
v = \sqrt{\frac{GM}{r}}
$$

## Kepler’s Third Law:
$$
T^2 \propto r^3
$$

## Worked Example
Satellite at $r = 2R_E$ has lower speed than at the surface.

## Common Mistakes
Confusing orbital speed with escape speed.
