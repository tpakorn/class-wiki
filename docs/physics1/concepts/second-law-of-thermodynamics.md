# Second Law of Thermodynamics

Entropy of an isolated system never decreases for real processes.

## Intuition
Ice cubes melt in warm water and never spontaneously re-form — that direction is irreversible.

## Formal Definition
Clausius statement: No process can transfer heat from cold to hot without work input.

Entropy change in reversible process:
$$
\Delta S = \int\frac{dQ}{T}
$$

## Worked Example
For reversible heat transfer, $\Delta S$ must be integrated along the actual path.

## Common Mistakes
Saying “entropy is disorder” without supporting it with the statistical-mechanical definition.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Can entropy decrease locally in an open system?
A: Yes — if it exports enough entropy to the environment.
