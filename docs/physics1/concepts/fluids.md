# Fluids

Fluid statics and dynamics use pressure, buoyancy, and Bernoulli’s principle.

## Intuition
A boat floats because it displaces enough water to balance its weight.

## Formal Definition
Pascal’s principle:
$$
P = \frac{F}{A}
$$

Archimedes:
$$
F_B = \rho_f V g
$$

## Worked Example
A $2\,\text{m}^3$ submerged object in water ($\rho=1000\,\text{kg/m}^3$) feels $19.6\,\text{kN}$ of buoyant force.

## Common Mistakes
Confusing displaced volume with object volume when partially submerged.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Why does a ship float despite being made of iron?
A: Its overall density, including enclosed air, is less than water.
