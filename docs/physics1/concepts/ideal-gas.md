# Ideal Gas

An ideal gas model describes a large number of point particles with elastic collisions.

## Intuition
Inflating a balloon: more gas particles inside push harder against the walls, raising pressure.

## Formal Definition
Equation of state:
$$
PV = N k_B T = nRT
$$

 Root-mean-square speed:
$$
v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3k_B T}{m}}
$$

## Worked Example
$2\,\text{mol}$ ideal gas at $300\,\text{K}$ in volume $0.05\,\text{m}^3$ gives $P \approx 99.8\,\text{kPa}$.

## Common Mistakes
Forgetting $n$ vs. $N$ and $R$ vs. $k_B$.
