# Rolling, Torque, and Angular Momentum

Rolling couples translation and rotation. Torque changes angular momentum.

## Intuition
A spinning bicycle wheel resists tilting due to angular momentum.

## Formal Definition
Torque:
$$
\vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F}
$$

Angular momentum:
$$
\vec{L} = \vec{r} \times \vec{p} = I\vec{\omega}
$$

## Worked Example
A force $10\,\text{N}$ at $0.3\,\text{m}$ perpendicular to the lever arm gives $\tau = 3\,\text{N m}$.

## Common Mistakes
Treating torque as a scalar instead of a cross product.

## Quiz Questions
Q: What produces torque?
A: A force applied off-center, perpendicular to the lever arm.
