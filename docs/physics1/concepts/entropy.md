Irreversible Processes and Entropy
Weird wrong-way situation
 Ex 1: if you were to wrap your hands around a cup of hot coffee.
your hands got cooler and the cup got warmer.
Ex 2: if you popped a helium balloon, all the helium molecules were
to gather together in the original shape of the balloon.
Total energy is not changed; but entropy changes.

Irreversible Processes and Entropy
Entropypostulate
· If an irreversible process occurs in a closed system, the entropy S of
the system always increases; it never decreases.
 Because of this property, the change in entropy is sometimes called
"the arrow of time.'
Ex 3: the explosion of a popcorn kernel, not the exploded popcorn
reforming the original kernel.
