# Projectile Motion

Projectile motion is 2D motion under constant gravity, separable into horizontal and vertical components.

## Intuition
Throw an object and gravity pulls it down while it continues moving forward — independent horizontal and vertical motion.

## Formal Definition
A projectile under gravity with negligible air resistance follows a parabola. Horizontal acceleration is zero; vertical acceleration is $-g$.

## Mathematical Formulation
$$
x(t) = v_0\cos\theta\; t, \quad y(t) = v_0\sin\theta\; t - \tfrac12 g t^2
$$

Range on level ground:
$$
R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin 2\theta}{g}
$$

## Worked Example
For $v_0 = 20\,\text{m/s}$ and $\theta = 45°$, $R = 40.8\,\text{m}$.

## Common Mistakes
Using total speed in the range formula without resolving $\theta$.
