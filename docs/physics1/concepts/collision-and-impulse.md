# Collision and Impulse

Impulse is the time-integrated force during a collision, producing momentum change.

## Intuition
A car crash durablity is improved by increasing the time over which momentum is changed.

## Formal Definition
Impulse:
$$
\vec{J} = \int \vec{F}\,dt = \Delta\vec{p}
$$

## Worked Example
A $0.1\,\text{kg}$ ball reversing from $+5$ to $-5\,\text{m/s}$ receives $\Delta p = -1.0\,\text{kg m/s}$.

## Common Mistakes
Ignoring vector directions in elastic collisions.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Does a longer collision time increase average force?
A: No — same impulse spread over more time reduces force.
