# Newton’s Laws

Newton’s three laws form the foundation of classical mechanics.

## Intuition
Objects keep doing what they were doing — unless a force changes that.

## Formal Definition
1. Inertia: an object remains at rest or in uniform straight-line motion unless acted on by a net force.
2. $\vec{F} = m\vec{a}$
3. Action and reaction are equal and opposite.

## Mathematical Formulation
$$
\sum \vec{F} = m\vec{a}
$$

## Worked Example
A $2\,\text{kg}$ block on a frictionless table pushed by $10\,\text{N}$ accelerates at $5\,\text{m/s}^2$.

## Common Mistakes
Thinking Newton’s second law is $F = mv$ instead of $F = ma$.

Q: If net force is zero, is velocity necessarily zero?
A: No — velocity is constant.
