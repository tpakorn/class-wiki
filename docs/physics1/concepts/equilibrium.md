# Equilibrium and Elasticity

Equilibrium requires zero net force and zero net torque.

## Intuition
A balanced seesaw with equal masses at equal distances stays horizontal.

## Formal Definition
Static equilibrium:
$$
\sum \vec{F} = 0, \quad \sum \vec{\tau} = 0
$$

Elasticity:
$$
\sigma = E\,\epsilon
$$

## Worked Example
A beam supported at two points with a load in the middle has reactions found from two equilibrium equations.

## Common Mistakes
Forgetting torque equilibrium in 2D beam problems.

## Quiz Questions
Q: Can an object be in translational equilibrium but not rotational equilibrium?
A: Yes — forces cancel but torques do not.
