# Damped and Forced Oscillations

Real oscillators lose energy to friction or driving forces.

## Intuition
A pendulum in air swings forever in theory, but air resistance slows it down — damping.

## Formal Definition
Damped oscillator:
$$
m\ddot{x} + b\dot{x} + kx = F_0\cos\omega_d t
$$

## Worked Example
Critical damping returns equilibrium fastest without overshoot.

## Common Mistakes
Ignoring phase difference in steady-state forced response.

## Quiz Questions
Q: What does critical damping mean?
A: System returns to equilibrium as quickly as possible without oscillating.
