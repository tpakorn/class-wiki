# SC133 · Physics for Engineers I

**A knowledge wiki for SC133** — Thammasat University, semester 1. Thirty lectures
from measurement to entropy: the complete first course in how the physical world
moves, collides, spins, flows, oscillates, and heats.

## The course in one paragraph

Mechanics is one long story told twice. First, instant by instant:
[kinematics](concepts/kinematics.md) describes motion,
[Newton's laws](concepts/newtons-laws.md) explain it, and applications pile up —
[projectiles](concepts/projectile-motion.md), [friction](concepts/friction-and-drag.md),
[circular motion](concepts/circular-motion.md), [rotation](concepts/rotation.md),
[gravity and orbits](concepts/keplers-laws.md). Then the same story via bookkeeping:
[energy](concepts/conservation-of-energy.md), [momentum](concepts/linear-momentum.md)
and [angular momentum](concepts/rolling-torque-angular-momentum.md) are *conserved*,
letting you skip the trajectory and compare before with after. The finale applies both
tellings to continuous matter: [fluids](concepts/fluids.md),
[oscillations](concepts/simple-harmonic-motion.md), [waves](concepts/waves.md), and
[thermodynamics](concepts/second-law-of-thermodynamics.md) — where mechanics meets
probability and time gets its arrow.
