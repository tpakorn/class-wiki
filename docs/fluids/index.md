## The course in one paragraph

Fluid mechanics is what happens when you take Newton's laws seriously for a substance
that cannot hold its shape. Starting from the [continuum hypothesis](concepts/what-is-a-fluid.md),
the course builds up statics ([pressure](concepts/pressure.md), [buoyancy](concepts/buoyancy.md)),
kinematics ([Eulerian vs Lagrangian views](concepts/eulerian-vs-lagrangian.md), the
[material derivative](concepts/material-derivative.md), the
[Reynolds transport theorem](concepts/reynolds-transport-theorem.md)), and dynamics —
[inviscid flow](equations/euler-equation.md), [potential flow](concepts/potential-flow.md),
and the [Navier–Stokes equation](equations/navier-stokes-equation.md). It closes with the
big phenomena: [shocks](concepts/shock-waves.md),
[instabilities](concepts/hydrodynamic-stability.md), and
[turbulence](concepts/turbulence.md).
