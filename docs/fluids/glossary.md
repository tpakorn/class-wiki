# Glossary · Fluid Mechanics

Alphabetical reference for every technical term used in the
[PC316 wiki](index.md). Each entry links to the page where the idea lives.

---

**Absolute pressure** — Pressure measured relative to perfect vacuum; always positive.
→ [Pressure](concepts/pressure.md)

**Archimedes' principle** — The buoyant force on a body equals the weight of fluid it
displaces. → [Buoyancy](concepts/buoyancy.md)

**Atwood number** — $\mathcal{A} = (\rho_1-\rho_2)/(\rho_1+\rho_2)$; sets the
Rayleigh–Taylor growth rate. → [Rayleigh–Taylor](concepts/rayleigh-taylor-instability.md)

**Barometric formula** — Pressure–height relation in a compressible atmosphere
(isothermal, lapse-rate, or adiabatic variants).
→ [Hydrostatic equilibrium](concepts/hydrostatic-equilibrium.md)

**Bernoulli's equation** — $p + \frac12\rho v^2 + \rho gz$ constant along a streamline
for steady inviscid incompressible flow. → [Bernoulli](equations/bernoulli-equation.md)

**Buckingham Pi theorem** — $n$ variables with $k$ dimensions reduce to $n-k$
dimensionless groups. → [Buckingham Pi](concepts/buckingham-pi-theorem.md)

**Buoyancy** — Net upward pressure force on a submerged body.
→ [Buoyancy](concepts/buoyancy.md)

**Center of pressure** — Point where the resultant hydrostatic force acts; lies below
the centroid. → [Forces on surfaces](concepts/hydrostatic-force-on-surfaces.md)

**Characteristics** — Curves $dx/dt = u \pm c_s$ along which information propagates in
compressible flow. → [Shock waves](concepts/shock-waves.md)

**Closure problem** — RANS averaging produces more unknowns (Reynolds stresses) than
equations. → [Reynolds averaging](concepts/reynolds-averaging.md)

**Complex potential** — $F(z) = \phi + i\psi$, analytic for 2-D potential flow.
→ [Conformal mapping](concepts/conformal-mapping-and-images.md)

**Conformal mapping** — Analytic change of complex variable carrying potential-flow
solutions between domains. → [Conformal mapping](concepts/conformal-mapping-and-images.md)

**Continuity equation** — Differential statement of mass conservation.
→ [Continuity](equations/continuity-equation.md)

**Continuum hypothesis** — Treating matter as smooth fields, valid when $L \gg \lambda$.
→ [What is a fluid?](concepts/what-is-a-fluid.md)

**Control volume** — Fixed region of space used for conservation bookkeeping.
→ [Reynolds transport theorem](concepts/reynolds-transport-theorem.md)

**Couette flow** — Linear shear flow between a fixed and a moving plate.
→ [Example](examples/couette-flow.md)

**Dimensional analysis** — Deducing functional forms by requiring unit consistency.
→ [Dimensional analysis](concepts/dimensional-analysis.md)

**Dynamic similarity** — Model and prototype share all relevant dimensionless groups.
→ [Buckingham Pi](concepts/buckingham-pi-theorem.md)

**Eddy viscosity** — Modeled turbulent momentum diffusivity $\nu_t$ (Boussinesq
hypothesis). → [Reynolds averaging](concepts/reynolds-averaging.md)

**Energy cascade** — Transfer of turbulent kinetic energy from large to small eddies.
→ [Energy cascade](concepts/energy-cascade.md)

**Eulerian description** — Fields observed at fixed points in space.
→ [Eulerian vs Lagrangian](concepts/eulerian-vs-lagrangian.md)

**Gauge pressure** — Pressure relative to local atmosphere.
→ [Pressure](concepts/pressure.md)

**Hagen–Poiseuille law** — $Q = \pi R^4 \Delta P / 8\mu L$ for laminar pipe flow.
→ [Example](examples/hagen-poiseuille-flow.md)

**Hydrostatic equilibrium** — Balance $\nabla p = \rho\mathbf{g}$ in a fluid at rest.
→ [Hydrostatic equilibrium](concepts/hydrostatic-equilibrium.md)

**Incompressible flow** — $\nabla\cdot\mathbf{v} = 0$; volume-preserving fluid motion.
→ [Continuity](equations/continuity-equation.md)

**Inertial range** — Scales where energy cascades without dissipation; $E(k)\sim k^{-5/3}$.
→ [Energy cascade](concepts/energy-cascade.md)

**Inviscid flow** — Idealization with zero viscosity, governed by Euler's equation.
→ [Euler's equation](equations/euler-equation.md)

**Irrotational flow** — $\nabla\times\mathbf{v} = 0$; admits a velocity potential.
→ [Potential flow](concepts/potential-flow.md)

**Isentropic flow** — Adiabatic and reversible; $P/\rho^\gamma$ constant.
→ [Bernoulli](equations/bernoulli-equation.md)

**Joukowski transformation** — $w = z + c^2/z$; maps circles to airfoils.
→ [Conformal mapping](concepts/conformal-mapping-and-images.md)

**Kelvin–Helmholtz instability** — Shear-driven interface instability.
→ [Kelvin–Helmholtz](concepts/kelvin-helmholtz-instability.md)

**Kinematic viscosity** — $\nu = \mu/\rho$; diffusivity of momentum (m²/s).
→ [Viscosity](concepts/viscosity.md)

**Knudsen number** — $\lambda/L$; gauges validity of the continuum hypothesis.
→ [What is a fluid?](concepts/what-is-a-fluid.md)

**Kolmogorov microscale** — $\eta = (\nu^3/\varepsilon)^{1/4}$, where turbulence
dissipates. → [Energy cascade](concepts/energy-cascade.md)

**Lagrangian description** — Following individual fluid particles.
→ [Eulerian vs Lagrangian](concepts/eulerian-vs-lagrangian.md)

**Laminar flow** — Smooth, layered, low-Reynolds-number flow.
→ [Reynolds number](concepts/reynolds-number.md)

**Laplace's equation** — $\nabla^2\phi = 0$; governs potential flow.
→ [Laplace](equations/laplace-equation.md)

**Mach number** — $M = u/c_s$; flow speed over sound speed.
→ [Shock waves](concepts/shock-waves.md)

**Material derivative** — $D/Dt = \partial_t + \mathbf{v}\cdot\nabla$; rate of change
following the fluid. → [Material derivative](concepts/material-derivative.md)

**Method of images** — Enforcing wall boundary conditions with mirror singularities.
→ [Conformal mapping](concepts/conformal-mapping-and-images.md)

**Navier–Stokes equation** — Momentum equation for viscous Newtonian fluids.
→ [Navier–Stokes](equations/navier-stokes-equation.md)

**Newtonian fluid** — Shear stress proportional to shear rate; constant $\mu$.
→ [Viscosity](concepts/viscosity.md)

**No-slip condition** — Fluid velocity equals wall velocity at a solid boundary.
→ [Viscosity](concepts/viscosity.md)

**Pathline** — Trajectory of an individual fluid particle.
→ [Eulerian vs Lagrangian](concepts/eulerian-vs-lagrangian.md)

**Potential flow** — Irrotational, incompressible flow described by $\nabla^2\phi = 0$.
→ [Potential flow](concepts/potential-flow.md)

**Rankine–Hugoniot conditions** — Conservation-law jump relations across a shock.
→ [Rankine–Hugoniot](equations/rankine-hugoniot-conditions.md)

**Rayleigh–Taylor instability** — Buoyancy-driven instability of heavy fluid over
light. → [Rayleigh–Taylor](concepts/rayleigh-taylor-instability.md)

**Reynolds decomposition** — Splitting fields into mean + fluctuation.
→ [Reynolds averaging](concepts/reynolds-averaging.md)

**Reynolds number** — $Re = UL/\nu$; inertia vs viscosity.
→ [Reynolds number](concepts/reynolds-number.md)

**Reynolds stress** — $\overline{u_i'u_j'}$; turbulent momentum flux appearing in RANS.
→ [Reynolds averaging](concepts/reynolds-averaging.md)

**Reynolds transport theorem** — Converts material-volume rates to control-volume
terms. → [RTT](concepts/reynolds-transport-theorem.md)

**Riemann invariants** — $C_\pm = u \pm \frac{2}{\gamma-1}c_s$, constant along
characteristics. → [Shock waves](concepts/shock-waves.md)

**Sedov–Taylor solution** — Self-similar strong blast wave, $R \propto (Et^2/\rho)^{1/5}$.
→ [Trinity example](examples/trinity-blast-wave.md)

**Shock wave** — Thin nonlinear front with abrupt jumps in $\rho, p, T, u$.
→ [Shock waves](concepts/shock-waves.md)

**Stream function** — $\psi$ with $u = \partial_y\psi$, $v = -\partial_x\psi$; contours
are streamlines. → [Potential flow](concepts/potential-flow.md)

**Streakline** — Locus of particles that passed a fixed point (dye line).
→ [Eulerian vs Lagrangian](concepts/eulerian-vs-lagrangian.md)

**Streamline** — Curve everywhere tangent to the instantaneous velocity field.
→ [Eulerian vs Lagrangian](concepts/eulerian-vs-lagrangian.md)

**Surface tension** — Interface energy per area; stabilizes short-wavelength
disturbances. → [Interface dispersion relation](equations/interface-dispersion-relation.md)

**Turbulence** — Chaotic, multi-scale, strongly mixing flow regime at high $Re$.
→ [Turbulence](concepts/turbulence.md)

**Turbulent kinetic energy (TKE)** — $k = \frac12\overline{u_i'u_i'}$; budget governed
by the TKE equation. → [TKE equation](equations/tke-transport-equation.md)

**Velocity potential** — Scalar $\phi$ with $\mathbf{v} = \nabla\phi$ in irrotational
flow. → [Potential flow](concepts/potential-flow.md)

**Venturi effect** — Pressure drop where a flow accelerates through a constriction.
→ [Pressure](concepts/pressure.md)

**Viscosity (dynamic)** — $\mu$; internal friction coefficient, Pa·s.
→ [Viscosity](concepts/viscosity.md)

**Vortex sheet** — Surface of discontinuous tangential velocity; infinite vorticity.
→ [Kelvin–Helmholtz](concepts/kelvin-helmholtz-instability.md)

**Vorticity** — $\boldsymbol{\omega} = \nabla\times\mathbf{v}$; local spin of fluid
elements. → [Potential flow](concepts/potential-flow.md)
